# Sky Goodies — Brand Direction Reset

> Research brief and proposed visual direction for the website revamp, after founder feedback that the basil-style wireframe felt off-brand.

---

## 1. What went wrong with the basil direction

**yourbasil.com** is a kids' lunchbox brand: bold, saturated, pebble-shaped, high-energy, confident. It works for a playful FMCG kids' brand you see at a supermarket shelf.

Sky Goodies is **not that brand.** It's:
- NID-trained designer-founded (2013)
- Featured in **Vogue, Elle Decor, Lonely Planet, Brand Shanghai**
- Vintage-inspired + whimsical + Indian
- About **the joy of making** — slow, mindful, handcrafted
- Sold to multi-age, design-aware buyers (not just kids)

Basil's aesthetic signals *"mass-market kids brand"* — the opposite of Sky Goodies' **"small design studio quiet brand"** identity.

---

## 2. What the existing Sky Goodies brand actually feels like

After studying the live site, IG, and press coverage, Sky Goodies' DNA:

| Attribute | Expression |
|---|---|
| **Whimsical** | Cloud-logo with tiny hot-air-balloon; dusty orange; sketchy hand-lettered headings |
| **Vintage** | Typewriter calendars, sewing-machine calendars, matchbook notebooks, gramophone kits |
| **Indian-rooted** | Ganesha idols, Krishna kits, Truck-Art-inspired patterns, kandils, Diwali décor |
| **Editorial** | Press-worthy photography, magazine-quality product shots, story-led |
| **Slow & mindful** | "Simple joys in a complex world :)" tagline; craft as meditation |
| **Ageless** | 7-year-olds AND 70-year-olds buy it; gift-worthy |
| **Eco-conscious** | FSC paper, biodegradable, zero-plastic — quietly stated, not shouted |

The brand lives in the same world as:
- **Chumbak** (Indian whimsical, but Chumbak is more consumer-retail)
- **Nicobar** (warm modern India premium)
- **Anthropologie** (warm, vintage, storybook)
- **Papier Tigre** (French paper brand with heart)
- **Smock Paper** (artisanal stationery)
- **Paper Source** (elevated craft)
- **Poketo** (Japanese-warm design)

NOT in the world of:
- yourbasil (kids FMCG)
- Mamaearth (mass-market D2C)
- boAt (volume/performance)
- Cult.fit (bold youth)

### 3.2 Nicobar (nicobar.com) — modern India premium

- Editorial photography — lifestyle-first, product-second
- Serif display + sans body
- Large, quiet, confident

### 3.3 Anthropologie's home/gift sections
- Storybook feel — each category has a mini-identity
- Hand-drawn illustrations sprinkled in
- Warm rich color palette
- Editorial, magazine-like section layouts

### 3.4 Poketo (poketo.com)
- Clean modern design with warm Japanese-minimal touches
- Restrained color (one accent at a time)
- Subtle animation
- No hard lines

## 4. The proposed direction: **"Storybook Studio"**

A warm, vintage-inspired, Indian-rooted, editorial paper brand — designed like a beloved independent bookshop, not a kids' shelf.

### 4.1 Five words
**Warm. Whimsical. Rooted. Quiet. Crafted.**

### 4.2 Design principles

1. **White space > loud blocks** — let products and photography breathe
2. **Soft lines > hard borders** — 1px hairlines, no 2px ink frames
3. **Editorial photography** — real use, warm light, stories not specs
4. **Typography with personality** — Fraunces italic display + handwritten accents + Nunito body
5. **Restrained color** — one accent at a time (orange, or a single muted tone)
6. **Illustration as texture** — hand-drawn icons, subtle patterns, tiny delightful details
7. **Indian-rooted but globally legible** — mentions Mumbai studio, Truck-Art, festivals, without being caricatured
8. **Price as quiet information** — not shouted in red boxes

### 4.3 Color palette — narrowed

**Primary:**
- `#FAF6F0` Cream (bg) — existing
- `#FDFCFA` Warm white — cards
- `#E8845A` Brand orange (accents, CTAs) — existing
- `#D26939` Deep orange (hover) — existing
- `#3D3833` Deep warm brown (body text) — existing
- `#1E1B18` Ink (headings)

**Support — use sparingly:**
- `#FCEEE6` Peach (section alt bg)
- `#EAD9B9` Dusty sand (secondary accent)
- `#A8B896` Sage (earthy green — for eco / Safe Eco section)
- `#B84D3C` Terracotta (Indian accent)
- `#D4A853` Gold (stars, quiet premium)

**What's removed from basil:**
- ❌ Lime green `#C8E616`
- ❌ Hot yellow `#F5CF8A` primary blocks
- ❌ Hot pink `#F4BBA4`
- ❌ 2px hard black borders
- ❌ Block-shadow hover effects

### 4.4 Typography

**Display — Fraunces**
Warm variable serif with personality. Used at:
- 400 italic for "The Coolest" upper-line
- 600 regular for main headings (not 900 — too bold)
- Never ALL CAPS at hero scale

**Accent — Caveat** (or similar handwritten)
Used *sparingly* for:
- "hello!", "psst!", tag lines on badges
- Handwritten-style annotations in blog posts
- Sketchy "made in Mumbai" scribbles

**Body — Nunito or DM Sans**
400-500 for body, 600-700 for emphasis. Not 800-900 bold.

### 4.5 Components

**Buttons:**
- Primary: solid orange `#E8845A` with white text, subtle rounded (8–14px radius), hover = deep orange `#D26939` + gentle 1–2px lift
- Secondary: outlined `1.5px` in brown with warm-white fill, hover = fills with brown
- NO chunky 2px borders, NO block shadows

**Cards:**
- No borders, no colored backgrounds
- Just product image on cream/white bg + centered title + price + subtle "Save X%" circular orange badge (top-right, small)
- Hover: image zooms 1.03× only

**Pills / filters:**
- Outlined thin, rounded
- Active = filled in warm tone (peach or sand)

**Section headings:**
- Small italic pre-line ("The Coolest")
- Bigger serif main line ("Must-Have Calendars")
- Paired with a small orange CTA link ("VIEW ALL →")

### 4.6 Layout patterns

**Hero:**
Editorial full-width photograph (lifestyle, not studio-cut) with small overlay content box — NOT huge stacked text. Subtle slideshow if needed.

**Categories:**
Circular frames (like existing site) — 5-6 across, each a category with a photo in a warm circle + thin orange text label below.

**Product grid:**
Clean 4-col (desktop) grid. White product backgrounds. Centered text. Small discount badges. Orange "Add to Cart" button appears on hover (or sits quietly below).

**Story section:**
Editorial magazine-spread feel — half image of Amit & Misha in studio, half story copy with warm serif + body.

**Trust/Eco strip:**
Hand-drawn illustrated icons (like existing site) on warm cream bg — not bold circle stamps.

**Testimonials:**
Not polaroid-chunky. Clean editorial quote cards — maybe a horizontal scroller with one quote at a time, large serif quote mark, reviewer name in italic.

**Instagram:**
Simple 6-up grid, thin-line IG icon overlays on hover, no chunky borders. The Insta Feed app drops in here naturally.

**Footer:**
Warm cream, generous, 4-col, subtle hairline dividers. No chunky pills.
